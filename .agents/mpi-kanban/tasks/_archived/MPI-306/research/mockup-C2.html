Head Swap

Takes the head from one image and places it onto the body in another, matching lighting and skin tone.

Works best when both faces are clearly lit and roughly front-on. Hard shadows across the face, heavy motion blur, or a head turned past three-quarters will fight the model.

You don't need to crop anything first — you'll mark the regions in the next two steps.

Mark where the new head goes

Box the head you want replaced. Include the hair and the jaw — the model needs the full silhouette to blend the edges.

Mark which head to take

Box the head to copy from. Already a tight portrait? Leave it as it is — the box starts on the whole image. This is here so you can drop in a full photo and take just the head.
